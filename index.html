Matt Nida has worked on format development for ITV, Avalon, Crackit Productions and more, originating and shaping ideas with originality and a sharp sense of humour. He’s the creative mind who convinced the world Joe Lycett had shredded £10k, helped plan Al Murray’s 2015 general election campaign, and even engineered the triumphant return of Mr Blobby in a moment that went gloriously viral.

With a knack for turning sparks of inspiration into bold, funny and memorable concepts, Matt brings fresh energy and inventive thinking to every project.

Matt Nida has over two decades of production experience across TV, radio, podcasts, branded content, live events, and experimental media platforms.

He’s produced high-profile entertainment shows such as the BAFTA-winning Joe Lycett vs David Beckham and The Big Fat Quiz of the Year, bringing together sharp creative vision, tight editorial control and audience-first ideas.

Beyond television, Matt has overseen some of the more ambitious stunts and activations in recent years (including Joe Lycett’s infamous money-shredding stunt in 2022), plus branded content and live formats that push boundaries in both experience and impact. He's produced sell-out live shows including Rob Brydon, Lee Mack & David Mitchell: Town To Town Live and John Robins & Elis James: Made Up Games Live, and has worked with some of British comedy and entertainment's biggest names.

His production approach is hands-on and collaborative, combining strong storytelling instinct with rigorous production craftsmanship — from format development through to post-production — to deliver work that’s both distinctive and resonant.
